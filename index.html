About Galileo Galilei

Galileo Galilei was born on 15 February 1564 at Pisa, Duchy of Florence, commonly referred to as Galileo, was an Italian astronomer, physicist and engineer, sometimes described as a polymath, from the city of Pisa, then part of the Duchy of Florence. Galileo has been called the "father" of observational astronomy, modern physics, the scientific method, and modern science. He took his education from "The University Of Pisa". He was known for Analytical Dynamics, Heliocentrism, Kinematics & Observational Astronomy. He was died on 8 January 1642 (aged 77), at Arcetri, Grand Duchy of Tuscany.
